<h2><a href="https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/problems/stickler-theif-1587115621/1">Stickler Thief</a></h2><h3>Difficulty Level : Difficulty: Medium</h3><hr><div class="problems_problem_content__Xm_eO"><p><span style="font-size: 18px;"><span style="font-size: 18px;">Stickler</span><span style="font-size: 18px;"> the thief</span><span style="font-size: 18px;"> wants to loot money from the houses arranged in a line. He cannot loot <strong>two consecutive </strong>houses and aims to <strong>maximize</strong> his total loot. <br></span></span><span style="font-size: 18px;"><span style="font-size: 18px;">Given an array, <strong>arr[]</strong> where arr[i] represents the amount of money in the i-th house.<br>Determine the <strong>maximum</strong> amount he can loot.</span></span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: 18px;"><strong>Examples:</strong></span></p>
<pre><span style="font-size: 18px;"><strong>Input: </strong>arr[] = [6, 5, 5, 7, 4]
<strong>Output: </strong>15
<strong>Explanation: </strong>Maximum amount he can get by looting 1st, 3rd and 5th house. Which is 6 + 5 + 4 = 15.</span></pre>
<pre><span style="font-size: 18px;"><strong>Input: </strong>arr[] = [1, 5, 3]
<strong>Output: </strong>5
<strong>Explanation: </strong>Loot only 2nd house and get maximum amount of 5.<br></span></pre>
<pre><span style="font-size: 14pt;"><strong>Input: </strong>arr[] = [4, 4, 4, 4]
<strong>Output: </strong>8
<strong>Explanation: </strong>The optimal choice is to loot every alternate house. Looting the 1st and 3rd houses, or the 2nd and 4th, both give a maximum total of 4 + 4 = 8.</span></pre>
